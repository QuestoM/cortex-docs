## 1. Purpose

This Confidentiality Policy defines the obligations and controls that protect confidential information belonging to Questo Ltd, its customers, partners, and employees. It establishes requirements for non-disclosure agreements, information handling, tenant data isolation within the corteX AI Agent SDK, and the treatment of confidential information throughout its lifecycle.

Given that corteX operates as a multi-tenant AI agent SDK where customer prompts, API keys, agent configurations, and operational data flow through the platform, robust confidentiality controls are essential to maintaining customer trust and regulatory compliance.

**SOC 2 Mapping**: Confidentiality TSC (C1.1-C1.2), CC6.1 (Logical Access), CC6.5 (Data Restrictions)

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

- **Personnel**: All employees, contractors, consultants, and third parties who have access to Questo Ltd confidential information in any form.
- **Information**: All non-public information created, received, stored, transmitted, or processed by Questo Ltd, including but not limited to: source code, customer data, tenant configurations, API keys, business strategies, financial information, employee data, and intellectual property.
- **Systems**: All systems that store, process, or transmit confidential information, including development environments, cloud infrastructure, communication platforms, and the corteX SDK itself.
- **Tenant Data**: All data belonging to or generated by corteX SDK tenants, including prompts, agent outputs, memory contents, decision traces, and audit logs.

## 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Confidential Information** | Any information classified as INTERNAL, CONFIDENTIAL, or RESTRICTED per POL-008 |
| **NDA** | Non-Disclosure Agreement; a legally binding contract that establishes confidentiality obligations between parties |
| **Tenant Data** | All data belonging to a corteX SDK customer, including prompts, outputs, configurations, API keys, and operational logs |
| **Data Classification** | The process of categorizing information based on sensitivity, enforced programmatically by `corteX/security/classification.py` |
| **DataLevel** | The enumeration (PUBLIC, INTERNAL, CONFIDENTIAL, RESTRICTED) defined in `corteX/security/classification.py` used to classify data at runtime |
| **KeyVault** | The corteX security module (`corteX/security/vault.py`) that provides per-tenant API key isolation with Fernet encryption (AES-128-CBC + HMAC-SHA256, PBKDF2-derived 256-bit key) |
| **BYOK** | Bring-Your-Own-Key; the model where tenants supply their own LLM API keys, which Questo never accesses in production |
| **Data Custodian** | The individual responsible for implementing controls to protect a specific category of confidential information |

## 4. Roles and Responsibilities

### 4.1 CTO (Policy Owner)

- Owns this policy and ensures its implementation across all operations
- Approves access to CONFIDENTIAL and RESTRICTED information
- Reviews and approves confidentiality controls for new product features
- Ensures tenant data isolation is maintained in the corteX architecture

### 4.2 Security Lead

- Manages the NDA lifecycle (issuance, tracking, renewal, archival)
- Conducts periodic reviews of confidentiality controls and data handling practices
- Investigates suspected confidentiality breaches and coordinates response
- Maintains the confidential information inventory

### 4.3 All Personnel

- Sign NDAs before receiving access to confidential information
- Handle confidential information according to its classification level
- Report suspected confidentiality breaches immediately per POL-004
- Return or destroy all confidential information upon termination of employment or contract
- Never discuss confidential information in public settings or over unsecured channels

## 5. Policy Statements

### 5.1 Confidential Information Categories

The following categories of information are treated as confidential at Questo Ltd:

| Category | Classification | Examples |
|----------|---------------|----------|
| **Tenant Data** | RESTRICTED | Customer prompts, agent outputs, API keys, tenant configurations |
| **Source Code** | CONFIDENTIAL | corteX SDK source code, proprietary algorithms, brain-inspired engine logic |
| **Business Information** | CONFIDENTIAL | Financial data, strategic plans, pricing, investor communications |
| **Employee Data** | CONFIDENTIAL | Personnel records, compensation, performance reviews |
| **Security Information** | RESTRICTED | Encryption keys, vulnerability reports, penetration test results, incident details |
| **Partner Information** | CONFIDENTIAL | Information received under NDA from partners, vendors, or prospects |

### 5.2 Non-Disclosure Agreements

#### 5.2.1 Employee and Contractor NDAs

- All employees must sign a confidentiality agreement as part of their employment contract before their start date.
- All contractors and consultants must sign an NDA before receiving access to any Questo Ltd systems or information.
- NDAs must include: definition of confidential information, obligations of the receiving party, exclusions, term and survival period (minimum 2 years post-termination), and remedies for breach.

#### 5.2.2 Third-Party NDAs

- NDAs must be executed with all third parties before sharing any CONFIDENTIAL or RESTRICTED information.
- The CTO approves all third-party NDA terms. Non-standard NDA terms require legal review.
- A registry of active NDAs is maintained by the Security Lead, including party names, effective dates, expiration dates, and scope.

#### 5.2.3 Customer Confidentiality

- Customer data processed by the corteX SDK is treated as RESTRICTED by default.
- Customer terms of service include mutual confidentiality obligations.
- Questo Ltd does not access, view, or analyze customer tenant data in production on-premises deployments. The BYOK model ensures Questo has no access to customer LLM API keys.

### 5.3 Tenant Data Isolation

The corteX SDK enforces confidentiality through architectural isolation:

1. **Memory Isolation**: Working memory, short-term memory, long-term memory, and episodic memory are scoped to individual tenant sessions. Cross-tenant memory access is architecturally impossible.
2. **KeyVault Isolation**: Each tenant's API keys are stored in a tenant-scoped `KeyVault` instance (`corteX/security/vault.py`) with Fernet encryption (AES-128-CBC + HMAC-SHA256, PBKDF2-derived 256-bit key). No tenant can access another tenant's keys.
3. **Audit Log Isolation**: Audit entries in `AuditStream` (`corteX/observability/audit_stream.py`) are tagged with `tenant_id`. Queries are tenant-filtered by default.
4. **Decision Trace Isolation**: `DecisionTracer` (`corteX/observability/tracer.py`) traces are scoped to `tenant_id`, preventing cross-tenant decision visibility.
5. **Data Classification Enforcement**: `DataClassifier` (`corteX/security/classification.py`) classifies data at runtime. CONFIDENTIAL and RESTRICTED data is restricted to on-premises models only, preventing accidental leakage to external LLM providers.

### 5.4 Information Handling Requirements

| Classification | Storage | Transmission | Sharing | Disposal |
|---------------|---------|-------------|---------|----------|
| **RESTRICTED** | Encrypted at rest (AES-256), access logged | TLS 1.2+ only | CTO approval required, recipient NDA required | Cryptographic erasure or certified destruction |
| **CONFIDENTIAL** | Encrypted at rest, access controlled | TLS 1.2+ only | Manager approval, recipient NDA required | Secure deletion |
| **INTERNAL** | Access controlled | Encrypted channels preferred | Internal personnel only | Standard deletion |
| **PUBLIC** | No restrictions | No restrictions | No restrictions | No restrictions |

### 5.5 Confidentiality in AI Agent Operations

- Tenant prompts are never logged in full unless the tenant explicitly enables detailed audit logging via `AuditConfig`.
- The `SafetyPolicy` module prevents agent outputs from containing leaked API keys, using `KeyVault.detect_leak()` to sanitize outputs automatically.
- LLM provider selection respects data classification: CONFIDENTIAL and RESTRICTED data is routed only to on-premises or tenant-approved providers.
- Agent memory is purged at session end unless the tenant configures persistent memory with appropriate retention policies.

### 5.6 Confidentiality Breach Response

- Suspected confidentiality breaches are reported immediately per POL-004 (Incident Response Plan).
- The Security Lead coordinates breach investigation, including determining the scope of information exposed and the parties affected.
- Affected parties (customers, employees, partners) are notified within 72 hours of confirmed breach, or sooner if required by applicable law.
- Post-incident review identifies root cause and implements corrective measures to prevent recurrence.

## 6. Procedures

### 6.1 NDA Management Procedure

1. The Security Lead maintains the NDA registry with: party name, agreement type (employee/contractor/third-party), effective date, expiration date, scope, and digital copy of the signed agreement.
2. NDAs are reviewed 60 days before expiration. Renewal or termination decisions are documented.
3. Upon termination of an NDA relationship, the Security Lead verifies that all confidential information has been returned or destroyed and obtains written confirmation.

### 6.2 Confidential Information Inventory

1. The Security Lead maintains an inventory of confidential information categories, their custodians, storage locations, and applicable controls.
2. The inventory is reviewed annually during the policy review cycle.
3. New categories of confidential information are added to the inventory as they are identified.

### 6.3 Annual Confidentiality Review

1. The CTO conducts an annual review of confidentiality controls, including: NDA compliance, data handling practices, tenant isolation mechanisms, and confidentiality breach history.
2. Findings are documented and remediation actions are tracked to completion.
3. The review results are reported to the CEO as part of the annual security posture report.

## 7. Exceptions

1. Exceptions to this policy require written approval from the CTO.
2. Exceptions involving RESTRICTED information require CEO approval.
3. Exception requests must include: the specific policy provision, business justification, compensating controls, and requested duration (maximum 12 months).
4. No exceptions are permitted for: NDA requirements for access to RESTRICTED data, tenant data isolation controls, or the prohibition on accessing customer tenant data in production.
5. All exceptions are documented in the Exception Register per POL-001 Section 7.

## 8. Enforcement

- Violations of this policy, including unauthorized disclosure of confidential information, may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment or contract.
- Violations involving willful disclosure of RESTRICTED information (customer data, encryption keys) will be referred to legal counsel and may result in civil or criminal proceedings.
- The Security Lead investigates all suspected confidentiality breaches.
- NDA violations by third parties are escalated to legal counsel for enforcement action.
